# 🚀 Artemis II: The Return of Humans to Lunar Orbit

**Artemis II** is the first crewed flight of NASA's Artemis program and the first time humans will visit the vicinity of the Moon since Apollo 17 in 1972.

## 📅 Mission Launch Info
*   **Launch Date:** Scheduled for **Wednesday, April 1, 2026** at **6:24 p.m. EDT**.
*   **Launch Site:** Kennedy Space Center, Florida (Launch Complex 39B).
*   **Duration:** Approximately **10 days**.

## 🌓 Mission Objectives
Artemis II is a critical test flight to verify Orion’s life-support systems. 
*   **Lunar Flyby:** A "figure-eight" trajectory around the far side of the Moon.
*   **System Validation:** Validating Orion's performance in deep space.
*   **Preparation for Landing:** Rehearsal for **Artemis III**, which will land humans on the Moon.

## 🧑‍🚀 The Crew
An international crew representing historic milestones:
1.  **Reid Wiseman (Commander):** Veteran NASA astronaut.
2.  **Victor Glover (Pilot):** First person of color on a lunar mission.
3.  **Christina Koch (Mission Specialist):** First woman on a lunar mission.
4.  **Jeremy Hansen (Mission Specialist, CSA):** First non-U.S. citizen to travel to the Moon.

## 🌌 Significance
Artemis II represents the rebirth of lunar exploration and the transition from Low Earth Orbit to deep space presence. This project, *Artemis: The Free Return*, is timed to coincide with this historic flight, allowing everyone to virtually sit in the commander's seat.
