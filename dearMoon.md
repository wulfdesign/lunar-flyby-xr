# 🌙 The dearMoon Project: A History of Vision and Schedule

The **dearMoon project** was a first-of-its-kind private lunar tourism mission, conceived and funded by Japanese entrepreneur **Yusaku Maezawa**. It aimed to take a crew of artists on a week-long journey around the Moon using SpaceX's Starship.

## 🚀 Key Milestones
*   **2018:** Announced by Elon Musk and Yusaku Maezawa. The mission shifted from a Dragon/Falcon Heavy flight to the massive **Starship** (BFR) to accommodate a larger crew of creatives.
*   **2021:** Global crew search began, attracting over 1 million applicants from 249 countries.
*   **2022:** Official crew announced, including **Steve Aoki**, **Tim Dodd (Everyday Astronaut)**, **T.O.P**, and other world-class artists.
*   **2023:** Original target launch date. Development of Starship continued at Starbase, Texas, but the timeline began to shift.

## 🛑 Cancellation (June 1, 2024)
On June 1, 2024, the project was officially cancelled. 

### **Primary Reasons:**
*   **Schedule Uncertainty:** Maezawa cited the lack of a clear timeline for Starship's readiness for crewed lunar flight. 
*   **Respect for Crew:** Maezawa expressed that he could not justify keeping the selected crew members in a state of professional and personal limbo indefinitely.

## 🕊️ Legacy
While the mission will not fly in its original form, *dearMoon* succeeded in sparking a global conversation about the intersection of art, space exploration, and the democratization of the cosmos. It served as a powerful catalyst for projects like *Artemis: The Free Return*, which seeks to fulfill the same "once-in-a-lifetime" vision through accessible technology.
